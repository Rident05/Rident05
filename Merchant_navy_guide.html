About the Merchant Navy

The Merchant Navy offers a world of adventure, travel, and a chance to work with global shipping companies. As a key part of the international shipping industry, it involves the transportation of goods and people across the seas. Whether you're interested in working as an officer, an engineer, or in a rating position, the Merchant Navy provides a variety of career paths.

Key Courses for Joining the Merchant Navy

Diploma in Nautical Science (DNS)

The DNS course is a one-year pre-sea training program that prepares candidates for a career as a deck officer. The course includes theoretical and practical training, followed by sea training that leads to obtaining a second mate's certificate.

GP Rating Course

The GP Rating course is designed to train candidates for entry-level positions as General Purpose Ratings. The course focuses on both deck and engine room tasks, providing a versatile role in the ship's operations.

Basic STCW Courses

The Basic STCW (Standards of Training, Certification, and Watchkeeping) courses are mandatory for all seafarers. These include basic safety training in fire prevention, personal survival techniques, first aid, and personal safety and social responsibilities.
